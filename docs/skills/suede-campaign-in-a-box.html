What it covers

  • Full campaign packages with seed, tease, announce, release-week, post-drop, fan proof, and catalog-afterlife phases.
  • Identity, era, and song-universe lanes for artist world, visual codes, symbols, wardrobe, language rules, and fan promise.
  • Hook, stunt, and ritual lanes for 5-15 second moments, fan missions, puzzles, timed drops, unlocks, and repeatable actions.
  • Visualizer, merch, and setlist lanes for lyric videos, canvas loops, stage loops, collector objects, set arcs, and crowd moments.
  • Catalog resurrection and collaborator lanes for demos, stems, live clips, anniversaries, outreach angles, asset exchange, and public proof.
  • Claim-safety checks that flag rights, sample, contributor, likeness, privacy, payment, platform-rule, and public-claim gaps.

When to use it

  • Use it when an artist team needs the whole rollout packaged into phases, owners, dependencies, copy, site sections, and fan actions.
  • Use it before bios, visuals, campaigns, or sites when the identity spine or release world is still fuzzy.
  • Use it to turn one track into hooks, scenes, captions, visual prompts, fan participation, object ideas, and a build-next CTA.
  • Use it to plan release stunts, fan rituals, visual treatments, merch objects, live-set arcs, catalog revival, or collaboration lanes.
  • Not for: sync pitching a track (use suede-sync-packaging).
  • Not for: organizing rights gaps (use suede-rights-audit).

Best prompt

Use $suede-campaign-in-a-box to package my next single into a full artist campaign with identity spine, era world, hook candidates, a release stunt, fan rituals, visualizer direction, merch object ideas, release-week calendar, email or DM copy, site sections, press angles, and rights or safety gaps marked unknown where facts are missing.
